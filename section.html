Jewelry for the neck ("tumarsha", "alka")

"Alka" is a chain with a pendant. This is a massive piece of jewelry, on the pendant of which various components were worn (for example, bells or coins). "Tumarsha" is an amulet that is worn around the neck. They were made with a void inside to carry prayer papers there. Most often, items were made of silver, triangular in shape. The Kazakhs believed that such amulets were able to protect their owner from any trouble.

Bracelet("bilezik")

Traditionally they were made of silver. National Kazakh bracelets were made of different types - solid or composite (formed from several parts). In the regions of Central Kazakhstan, thin bracelets dominated, in the southern regions - solid cast, and in the southeast - rounded and voluminous with inserts of precious stones.

Earrings("syrga")

This is the most widespread piece of jewelry in Kazakhstan; it is customary for girls to wear it at any age. There are many variations of this product: triangular, wire, ornamental flat earrings of various configurations. Earrings "ai sirga" - in the shape of the moon and "ayshykty sirga" - in the shape of a month have gained particular popularity. Very often, earrings are made with additional pendants from various gems. Massive, long and luxurious jewelry in the ears testified to the high status of the owner, to her significant role in society.
